# Primate Evolution and Taxonomy: Our Evolutionary Context

---

## What Are Primates?

### Defining Characteristics

**Primate Order:**
- Mammals
- ~500 species living
- Humans included

**Key Features (Trends):**
- Grasping hands/feet (opposable thumbs/big toes)
- Nails (not claws)
- Forward-facing eyes (stereoscopic vision, depth perception)
- Large brain (relative to body size)
- Extended life history (long gestation, childhood, lifespan)
- Reduced sense of smell
- Complex social behavior

**Not All Primates Have All:**
- Trends, not absolutes
- Variation enormous

---

## Primate Taxonomy

### Major Groups

**Order: Primates**

**Suborder: Strepsirrhini (Wet-nosed)**
- Lemurs (Madagascar)
- Lorises (Asia, Africa)
- More primitive features
- Nocturnal (many)
- Less social complexity

**Suborder: Haplorhini (Dry-nosed)**
- Tarsiers (Southeast Asia)
- Monkeys
- Apes
- Humans

**Within Haplorhini:**

**Platyrrhini (New World Monkeys):**
- Central/South America
- Flat noses
- Prehensile tails (some)
- Arboreal
- Examples: spider monkeys, howler monkeys, capuchins

**Catarrhini (Old World Monkeys and Apes):**
- Africa, Asia
- Downward-pointing noses
- No prehensile tails

**Split Into:**
- **Cercopithecoidea (Old World Monkeys)**: baboons, macaques, colobus
- **Hominoidea (Apes)**: lesser apes (gibbons), great apes, humans

---

## Hominoidea: The Apes

### Our Superfamily

**Characteristics:**
- No tail
- Larger brains
- More upright posture
- Longer arms
- Complex social behavior

**Lesser Apes:**
- **Hylobatidae**: Gibbons, siamangs
- Southeast Asia
- Small, arboreal
- Monogamous pairs
- Brachiators (arm-swinging)

**Great Apes (Hominidae):**

**Asian:**
- **Orangutans** (Pongo)
  - Sumatra, Borneo
  - Largest arboreal mammal
  - Mostly solitary
  - Highly intelligent

**African:**
- **Gorillas** (Gorilla)
  - Central/West Africa
  - Largest primates
  - Herbivorous
  - Silverback males lead groups

- **Chimpanzees** (Pan troglodytes)
  - Central/West Africa
  - Tool use
  - Hunting, war
  - Closest to humans (with bonobos)

- **Bonobos** (Pan paniscus)
  - Congo River basin
  - Female-dominant
  - Peaceful, sexual
  - Endangered

- **Humans** (Homo sapiens)
  - Global distribution
  - Bipedal
  - Massive brains
  - Language, culture

## Primate Origins

### When Did Primates Evolve?

**Timeline:**

**~65 Million Years Ago (mya):**
- Dinosaurs extinct (K-T extinction)
- Mammals diversify
- Primates emerge

**Paleocene-Eocene (~65-34 mya):**
- Early primates (Plesiadapiforms)
- Debate: true primates?
- Small, arboreal, insectivorous

**Eocene (~56-34 mya):**
- Adapoids (lemur-like)
- Omomyoids (tarsier-like)
- Europe, North America, Asia
- Warm climate

**Oligocene (~34-23 mya):**
- Cooling climate
- Primates retreat to tropics
- Egypt: Fayum deposits
  - **Aegyptopithecus** (early catarrhine)
  - Small, monkey-like
  - Ancestor of Old World monkeys and apes

---

## Ape Evolution (Miocene)

### The Age of Apes

**Miocene (~23-5 mya):**
- Apes radiate
- Africa, Europe, Asia
- Diverse species

**Early Miocene Africa:**
- **Proconsul** (~20 mya)
  - Kenya
  - Primitive ape
  - Arboreal, quadrupedal
  - No tail
  - Fruit-eater

**Middle-Late Miocene:**
- Apes widespread
- Then decline (competition from monkeys?)

**Important Species:**

**Sivapithecus (~12-8 mya):**
- South Asia
- Related to orangutans
- Thick enamel (hard foods)

**Gigantopithecus (~9 mya-100,000 years ago):**
- Asia
- Largest primate ever
- 3 meters tall, 500+ kg
- Extinct
- Only known from teeth, jaws (found in Chinese apothecaries!)

## Hominoid-Cercopithecoid Split

### Monkeys vs. Apes Diverge

**Molecular Clock:**
- DNA comparisons
- Estimate divergence times
- Mutations accumulate steadily

**Divergence ~25 mya:**
- Old World monkeys one line
- Apes (hominoids) another

**Why Apes Declined, Monkeys Succeeded?**
- Climate change (cooler, drier)
- Monkeys more adaptable?
- Better digestive system (bilophodont teeth)
- Open habitats favor monkeys

## Great Ape Divergences

### When Did Lineages Split?

**Molecular Evidence:**

**~18-16 mya:**
- Orangutan lineage splits from African apes + humans

**~10-8 mya:**
- Gorilla lineage splits

**~7-6 mya:**
- Chimpanzee/Bonobo lineage splits from human lineage
- **THIS IS WHEN HOMININS BEGIN**

**~2 mya:**
- Chimpanzees and bonobos split (recent!)

## Key Concepts in Evolutionary Theory

### Darwin's Contributions

**Charles Darwin (1859):**
- "On the Origin of Species"
- Natural selection
- Common descent

**Natural Selection:**
1. Variation (individuals differ)
2. Inheritance (traits passed on)
3. Differential reproduction (some reproduce more)
4. Adaptation (traits that help survival/reproduction spread)

**Evolution:**
- Change in allele frequencies over time
- Populations evolve, not individuals
- Not goal-directed

### Modern Synthesis

**1930s-40s:**
- Darwin + Genetics (Mendel)
- Population genetics
- Neo-Darwinism

**Mechanisms:**
- Natural selection
- Genetic drift (random)
- Gene flow (migration)
- Mutation (variation source)

## Dating Methods

### How We Know Ages

**Relative Dating:**
- Stratigraphy (layers)
- Older = deeper (usually)
- Biostratigraphy (index fossils)

**Absolute Dating:**

**Radiometric:**
- Radioactive decay
- Known half-lives
- Potassium-Argon (K-Ar)
  - Volcanic rock
  - Millions of years
- Carbon-14 (radiocarbon)
  - Organic material
  - <50,000 years

**Molecular Clock:**
- DNA mutation rate
- Estimate divergence times
- Calibrate with fossils

## Primate Phylogeny

### Evolutionary Tree

**Cladistics:**
- Group by shared derived traits
- Clades (monophyletic groups)
- Common ancestor + all descendants

**Our Clade:**
- Hominoidea (apes)
  - Hylobatidae (gibbons)
  - Hominidae (great apes)
    - Pongo (orangutans)
    - Homininae
      - Gorilla
      - Hominini (chimps, bonobos, humans)
        - Pan (chimps, bonobos)
        - **Hominina (human lineage, extinct relatives)**

**Hominina = Hominins:**
- Human lineage after split from chimps
- Includes extinct species
- Australopithecus, Homo, etc.

## Convergent Evolution vs. Homology

### Distinguishing Similarity

**Homology:**
- Shared from common ancestor
- Example: primate hands (five fingers)

**Analogy (Convergent Evolution):**
- Similar function, different origins
- Example: bird wings vs. bat wings

**Primate Features:**
- Grasping hands = homology (inherited)
- Bipedalism (humans) vs. quadrupedalism (chimps) = divergence

---

## Mosaic Evolution

### Features Evolve at Different Rates

**Not All-or-Nothing:**
- Some traits change quickly
- Others slowly
- Combinations

**Example:**
- Early hominins: bipedal but small brains
- Later: larger brains
- Traits appear at different times

## Environmental Context

### Climate and Habitat

**Climate Change Drives Evolution:**
- Miocene apes (warm, forested)
- Cooling, drying (Pliocene)
- Forests shrink
- Grasslands expand (savannas)
- Hominins emerge in changing Africa

**Variability Selection Hypothesis:**
- Rick Potts
- Climate instability (oscillations)
- Favors adaptability
- Generalists survive
