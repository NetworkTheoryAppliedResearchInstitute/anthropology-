# Hominin Timeline: From Australopithecus to Archaic Homo

---

## What Is a Hominin?

### Defining Our Lineage

**Hominin (Hominina):**
- Human lineage after split from chimpanzees
- ~7-6 million years ago
- Includes all extinct relatives
- Humans = only surviving species

**Key Feature:**
- **Bipedalism** (walking upright on two legs)
- Defines hominins
- Even before big brains

**Why Bipedalism?**
- Debated!
- Savanna hypothesis (see open distances)
- Thermoregulation (less sun exposure)
- Carrying (food, infants, tools)
- Energy efficiency
- Display, threat
- Likely multiple factors

## Earliest Possible Hominins (7-6 mya)

### Fragmentary Evidence

**Sahelanthropus tchadensis (~7-6 mya):**
- Chad, Central Africa
- "Toumaï" skull
- Small brain (~350 cc, chimp-sized)
- Foramen magnum (hole for spinal cord) forward
- Suggests bipedalism?
- Flat face
- **Debate:** Hominin or ape?

**Orrorin tugenensis (~6 mya):**
- Kenya
- Femur suggests bipedalism
- Small fragments
- "Millennium Man"

**Ardipithecus kadabba (~5.8-5.2 mya):**
- Ethiopia
- Toe bone suggests bipedalism
- Very fragmentary

**Caution:**
- All fragmentary
- Dating uncertain
- Hominin status debated
- Important: show early experiments

## Ardipithecus ramidus (4.4 mya)

### "Ardi"

**Discovery:**
- Ethiopia (Middle Awash)
- Tim White team
- Published 2009 (after 15 years study!)
- Most complete early hominin (before Lucy)

**Features:**
- Brain: ~300-350 cc (chimp-like)
- Bipedal (pelvis, feet)
- BUT: retained grasping big toe (still climbing)
- Teeth: small canines (unlike apes)
- Arms: long (ape-like)
- **Mosaic:** bipedal but not fully committed

**Habitat:**
- Woodland, not savanna
- Challenges savanna hypothesis
- Forests, not grasslands

**Implications:**
- Bipedalism before open habitats
- Our ancestors not chimp-like
- Chimps evolved too (knuckle-walking derived?)

## Australopithecus: The Southern Apes (4-2 mya)

### Gracile Australopithecines

**Genus Australopithecus:**
- "Southern ape"
- East/South Africa
- 4-2 million years ago
- Several species
- Small brains (400-550 cc)
- **Bipedal but not fully modern gait**
- Still climbing trees
- Small body size (1-1.5 m, 30-50 kg)

**Key Species:**

### Australopithecus anamensis (4.2-3.8 mya)

**Kenya, Ethiopia:**
- One of earliest
- Tibia shows bipedalism
- Ape-like skull

### Australopithecus afarensis (3.9-3.0 mya)

**"Lucy" and "Dikika Child":**

**Discovery:**
- Donald Johanson, 1974
- Hadar, Ethiopia
- "Lucy" = 40% complete skeleton
- 3.2 million years old
- Named after Beatles song "Lucy in the Sky with Diamonds"

**Features:**
- Small brain (~400-500 cc)
- Bipedal:
  - Pelvis wide, short
  - Femur angled (valgus knee)
  - Foramen magnum forward
- BUT: Long arms, curved fingers (climbing)
- Sexual dimorphism (males much larger)

**Laetoli Footprints (Tanzania, 3.6 mya):**
- Mary Leakey, 1976
- Volcanic ash preserved footprints
- Three individuals walked across
- **Proof of bipedalism**
- Modern-looking footprints
- But: big toe not fully adducted

**Dikika Child (Ethiopia, 3.3 mya):**
- Young A. afarensis
- 3-year-old
- Nearly complete
- Brain growth pattern
- Hyoid bone (speech?)

### Australopithecus africanus (3.3-2.1 mya)

**South Africa:**
- Taung Child (Raymond Dart, 1924)
- First australopith discovered
- Small brain (~450 cc)
- Bipedal
- Gracile build

**Debate:**
- Ancestral to Homo?
- Or side branch?
- Probably side branch

### Australopithecus garhi (2.5 mya)

**Ethiopia:**
- Near stone tools, butchered animals
- Tool use?
- Transitional to Homo?

## Robust Australopithecines (Paranthropus)

### Specialized Chewers

**Paranthropus ("Beside human"):**
- Or: Australopithecus (robust)
- 2.7-1.2 mya
- East/South Africa
- **NOT ancestral to humans**
- Side branch, extinct

**Features:**
- Massive jaws, teeth
- Sagittal crest (for chewing muscles)
- Small brains (~400-550 cc)
- Specialized diet (hard, tough foods)
- Seeds, nuts, roots

**Species:**

**Paranthropus aethiopicus (2.7-2.3 mya):**
- "Black Skull" (Kenya)
- Earliest robust
- Transitional

**Paranthropus boisei (2.3-1.2 mya):**
- "Nutcracker Man" (Louis Leakey, 1959)
- Olduvai Gorge, Tanzania
- Extreme: huge molars
- "Hyper-robust"

**Paranthropus robustus (2.0-1.2 mya):**
- South Africa
- Less extreme than boisei
- Still robust

**Extinction:**
- Outlasted by early Homo
- Competition?
- Climate change?
- ~1 million years ago

## Transition to Homo

### What Defines Homo?

**Criteria (Debated):**
- Larger brain (>600 cc)
- Smaller teeth, jaws
- Tool use (Oldowan tools)
- More modern limb proportions
- Longer legs, shorter arms

**Challenges:**
- Fuzzy boundary
- Variation
- Fragmentary fossils

---

## Early Homo (2.5-1.5 mya)

### First Members of Our Genus

**Homo habilis (2.4-1.4 mya):**

**"Handy Man":**
- Louis Leakey, 1960s
- Olduvai Gorge
- Associated with Oldowan stone tools
- Brain: ~600-700 cc
- Small body
- Long arms still

**Debate:**
- Is this Homo?
- Or late Australopithecus?
- Very variable (multiple species?)

**Homo rudolfensis (2.0-1.8 mya):**
- Kenya
- Larger brain (~750 cc)
- Flatter face
- Distinct from habilis?
- Or same species?
- Taxonomy messy

---

## Homo erectus (1.9 mya - 110,000 years ago)

### First Global Hominin

**Discovery:**
- Eugène Dubois, 1891
- Java, Indonesia
- "Java Man"
- Later: "Peking Man" (China)

**Features:**
- Brain: 600-1250 cc (average ~900 cc)
- **Much larger than earlier**
- Modern body proportions:
  - Long legs
  - Short arms
  - Tall (1.5-1.8 m)
  - Heavy brow ridge
  - Projecting face
  - No chin

**"Turkana Boy" (Kenya, 1.5 mya):**
- Nearly complete skeleton
- Adolescent male
- ~1.6 m tall
- Modern postcranial skeleton
- Small brain (~900 cc)

**Acheulean Tools:**
- Hand axes
- Symmetrical, bifacial
- More complex than Oldowan
- 1.7 mya - 100,000 years ago
- Spread with H. erectus

**First to Leave Africa:**
- Asia by 1.8 mya (Dmanisi, Georgia)
- Indonesia (Java)
- China (Zhoukoudian)
- Survived >1.5 million years!
- Last populations ~100,000 years ago (Java)

**Fire Use:**
- Evidence by ~1 mya
- Hearths (Zhoukoudian)
- Cooking
- Warmth, protection
- Social focus

**Variation:**
- Some call African erectus "Homo ergaster"
- Asian erectus "Homo erectus" (strict)
- Debate ongoing

## Archaic Homo Species

### Transitional Forms

**Homo heidelbergensis (700,000-200,000 years ago):**

**Africa and Europe:**
- Larger brains (1100-1400 cc)
- Less robust than erectus
- More modern face
- Tool: prepared-core technique

**Possible Ancestor:**
- Neanderthals (Europe)
- Modern humans (Africa)
- Maybe Denisovans (Asia)

**Homo naledi (335,000-236,000 years ago):**

**South Africa (Rising Star Cave):**
- Lee Berger, 2013
- 1500+ bones, 15+ individuals
- **Surprising:**
  - Small brain (~450-550 cc)
  - Primitive features
  - But: VERY recent dates
  - Overlaps with modern humans!
- Intentional burial?
- Deep cave

**Challenges Ideas:**
- Small brain, recent
- Multiple hominin species coexisted

## Flores "Hobbit" (Homo floresiensis)

### Enigmatic Island Dwarf

**Discovery:**
- Flores Island, Indonesia
- 2003
- ~100,000-50,000 years ago

**Features:**
- Tiny: ~1 meter tall
- Brain: ~400 cc (chimp-sized!)
- Primitive features
- But: made tools, hunted

**Explanations:**
- Island dwarfism (common in isolation)
- Descended from erectus?
- Or earlier hominin?
- Pathology? (microcephaly?) - debated, probably not

**Implications:**
- Multiple hominin species survived late
- Human evolution not simple linear progression

## Timeline Summary

**7-6 mya:** Sahelanthropus, Orrorin (possible earliest)
**5.8-4.4 mya:** Ardipithecus (Ardi)
**4-2 mya:** Australopithecus (Lucy, afarensis, africanus, garhi)
**2.7-1.2 mya:** Paranthropus (robust, extinct side branch)
**2.5-1.5 mya:** Early Homo (habilis, rudolfensis)
**1.9 mya-110 kya:** Homo erectus (first global, fire, Acheulean)
**700-200 kya:** Archaic Homo (heidelbergensis, transitional)
**335-236 kya:** Homo naledi (small brain, recent!)
**100-50 kya:** Homo floresiensis (island dwarf)

**Plus:**
- Neanderthals
- Denisovans
- Modern humans
- (See [[modern_human_origins.md]])

**Remember:** Hominins = human lineage after chimp split (~7-6 mya). Bipedalism defines hominins, appears before big brains. Earliest possible: Sahelanthropus, Orrorin (fragmentary). Ardi (4.4 mya) bipedal in woodlands. Australopithecus (4-2 mya): Lucy (A. afarensis), small brains, bipedal, still climbing, Laetoli footprints prove bipedalism. Paranthropus (robust, 2.7-1.2 mya) extinct specialized side branch. Early Homo (habilis, rudolfensis) ~2.5 mya, larger brains, tools. Homo erectus (1.9 mya-110 kya): modern body proportions, first to leave Africa, fire, Acheulean tools, survived >1.5 million years. Archaic Homo (heidelbergensis transitional, naledi surprisingly recent small-brained). Homo floresiensis ("hobbit") tiny island dwarf. Not linear progression—multiple species coexisted, branching tree. Understanding hominin timeline reveals human evolution as complex, bushy tree with many experiments, most extinct, only we remain.
