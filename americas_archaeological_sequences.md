# Americas Archaeological Sequences: From Arctic to Tierra del Fuego

---

## Mesoamerica: Land of Maize and Pyramids

### Geographic Extent

**Modern:**
- Central and Southern Mexico
- Guatemala, Belize
- Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua

**Environmental Diversity:**
- Highlands (volcanic)
- Lowlands (tropical rainforest)
- Coasts (Pacific, Caribbean)

---

## Mesoamerican Chronology

### Archaic Period (~7,000-2,000 BCE)

**Foragers → Farmers:**
- Domestication (maize from teosinte, beans, squash)
- Tehuacán Valley (Richard MacNeish)
- Gradual intensification

### Formative/Preclassic Period (~2,000 BCE - 250 CE)

### Early Formative (~2,000-1,000 BCE)

**Villages:**
- Sedentism
- Pottery
- Maize agriculture

### Middle Formative (~1,000-400 BCE)

**Olmec "Mother Culture":**
- Gulf Coast (Veracruz, Tabasco)
- **San Lorenzo** (~1,200-900 BCE)
- **La Venta** (~900-400 BCE)

**Colossal Heads:**
- Basalt (3+ meters, 20+ tons)
- Helmets or headdresses
- Portraits (individuals?)
- 17 known
- Transport (60+ km from source)

**Art Style:**
- "Were-jaguar" (human-jaguar hybrid)
- Jade, obsidian
- Symbols (later Maya elements)

**Ball Game:**
- Rubber balls
- Courts
- Ritual, political

**Influence:**
- Wide (Mesoamerica)
- Trade networks

### Late Formative (~400 BCE - 250 CE)

**Monte Albán (Oaxaca):**
- Zapotec
- Hilltop city
- Glyphs (early writing)
- Danzantes (carved stones, captives?)

---

### Classic Period (~250-900 CE)

**Peak:**
- Art, architecture, writing
- City-states
- Warfare

### Teotihuacan (~100 BCE - 550 CE)

**"Place Where the Gods Were Born":**
- Central Mexico (near modern Mexico City)
- Largest city in Americas
- **Population:** 100,000-200,000

**Layout:**
- Grid plan
- Avenue of the Dead (main axis)

**Pyramids:**
- **Pyramid of the Sun** (third largest in world)
  - 65 m high
  - Over Temple of the Feathered Serpent
- **Pyramid of the Moon**
- Temple of the Feathered Serpent (Quetzalcoatl)
  - Stone serpents
  - Sacrificial burials

**Economy:**
- Obsidian workshops
- Trade (wide network)
- Multiet

hnic (apartment compounds for different groups)

**Decline:**
- ~550 CE
- Burned (warfare? Internal revolt?)
- Influence persists

### Maya Civilization (~250-900 CE Classic, continues after)

**Geographic:**
- Lowlands (Petén, Yucatán)
- Highlands (Guatemala)

**City-States:**
- Tikal, Calakmul, Palenque, Copán, Caracol

**Monumental Architecture:**
- Pyramids (temples on top)
- Palaces
- Ball courts
- Stelae (carved stone monuments)

**Writing:**
- **Hieroglyphic script** (logosyllabic)
- Deciphered (Yuri Knorozov 1950s, Linda Schele 1970s+)
- Royal history, warfare

**Calendars:**
- **Long Count** (cycles, 5,125-year)
- Sacred calendar (260 days, Tzolk'in)
- Solar calendar (365 days, Haab)
- Astronomical observations (Venus, eclipses)

**Society:**
- Divine kingship
- Nobles, warriors, scribes
- Commoners, slaves

**Warfare:**
- Constant
- Capture for sacrifice
- Stelae depict battles
- Not "peaceful" (revision from decipherment)

**Classic Collapse (~800-900 CE):**

**Southern Lowlands:**
- Cities abandoned
- Stelae stop
- Population decline

**Theories:**
- **Drought** (paleoclimate evidence)
  - Severe, prolonged
  - Agricultural stress
- Overpopulation
- Deforestation
- Warfare
- Trade disruption
- Multivariate (cascading failures)

**Not "Disappearance":**
- Maya continue (northern cities: Chichen Itza, Uxmal)
- Descendants survive
- Spanish conquest (1520s)

### Postclassic Period (~900-1521 CE)

**Toltec (~900-1,150 CE):**
- Central Mexico (Tula)
- Militaristic
- Influence on Maya (Chichen Itza)

**Chichen Itza (~900-1,200 CE):**
- Northern Yucatán
- Toltec-Maya hybrid
- El Castillo (pyramid)
- Sacred Cenote (sacrifices)

**Mayapan (~1,200-1,450 CE):**
- Yucatán
- Walled city
- Political center

**Aztec Empire (~1,345-1,521 CE):**
- Central Mexico
- **Tenochtitlan** (capital, island in Lake Texcoco)
  - Founded 1,325 CE
  - 200,000+ people (1500s)
  - Chinampas ("floating gardens")
  - Causeways, aqueducts
  - Templo Mayor (Great Temple)

**Triple Alliance:**
- Tenochtitlan, Texcoco, Tlacopan

**Tribute Empire:**
- Conquered city-states
- Tribute (goods, labor)
- Not direct administration

**Religion:**
- Polytheistic
- Human sacrifice (extensive)
  - War captives
  - Feed sun
  - Huitzilopochtli (war god)

**Spanish Conquest:**
- Hernán Cortés (1519)
- Moctezuma II
- Disease, alliances (Tlaxcala)
- Fall of Tenochtitlan (1,521 CE)

## Andean South America

### Geographic Extent

**West Coast:**
- Peru, Bolivia (highlands, coast)
- Ecuador, Chile (parts)

**Environments:**
- **Coast** (desert, rich marine resources)
- **Highlands** (altiplano, valleys)
- **Montaña** (eastern slopes, Amazon)

---

## Andean Chronology

### Preceramic Period (~10,000-1,800 BCE)

**Early:**
- Foragers (coast, highlands)
- Marine resources (fish, shellfish)

**Late Preceramic (~3,000-1,800 BCE):**

**Norte Chico/Caral (~3,000-1,800 BCE):**
- **Caral** (largest site)
  - Coastal Peru (Supe Valley)
  - **Ruth Shady** (excavator, 1990s+)
  - Pyramids (stepped platforms)
  - **Oldest city in Americas?** (debated)
  - **Pre-ceramic** (before pottery!)
- Economy: fishing (anchovies), cotton, squash
- No maize
- Trade (coast ↔ highlands)
- Sunken circular plazas
- Quipu? (knotted strings, debated)

### Initial Period (~1,800-900 BCE)

**Pottery Introduced:**
- Ceramics appear
- Maize

**U-shaped Temples:**
- Coastal valleys

### Early Horizon (~900-200 BCE)

**Chavín de Huántar:**
- Highlands (Peru, Andes)
- Cult center (pilgrimage?)
- **Lanzón** (carved stone, deity)
- Feline, serpent, raptor iconography
- "Staff God" (widespread)
- Influence (Chavín style spreads)
- Not political empire (religious?)

### Early Intermediate Period (~200 BCE - 600 CE)

**Regional Cultures:**

**Nazca (South Coast):**
- **Nazca Lines** (geoglyphs)
  - Desert plateau
  - Lines, animals (spider, hummingbird, monkey), geometric
  - Visible from air
  - Purpose? (ritual pathways, water cult, astronomy?)
  - **Maria Reiche** (researcher, preservation)
- Polychrome pottery (elaborate)

**Moche (North Coast, ~100-800 CE):**
- **Huacas (pyramids):**
  - Huaca del Sol, Huaca de la Luna
  - Adobe bricks (millions)
- Irrigation (complex)
- **Metallurgy** (gold, silver, copper)
- **Lord of Sipán:**
  - Royal tomb (1987, Walter Alva)
  - Richest unlooted tomb (Americas)
  - Gold, silver, turquoise, shells
  - Sacrificed retainers
- Pottery (realistic, narrative scenes)
  - Sexual, warfare, ritual
- Warrior-priests

### Middle Horizon (~600-1,000 CE)

**Wari (Huari) Empire (~600-1,000 CE):**
- Highlands (central Peru)
- First expansionist state (Andes)
- Roads, administrative centers
- Orthogonal architecture (planned)
- Quipu (accounting)
- Decline (~1,000 CE)

**Tiwanaku (~300-1,000 CE):**
- **Altiplano** (Lake Titicaca, Bolivia/Peru border)
- Monumental (Gateway of the Sun)
- Raised-field agriculture
- Religious center
- Influence (southern Andes)
- Decline (~1,000 CE, drought?)

### Late Intermediate Period (~1,000-1,470 CE)

**Regional Kingdoms:**

**Chimú (~900-1,470 CE):**
- North Coast (Peru)
- **Chan Chan** (capital)
  - Largest adobe city (20 km²)
  - Walled compounds (ciudadelas)
  - Elite residences, storage
- Metallurgy (mass production)
- Conquered by Inka (1,470s)

### Late Horizon (~1,470-1,532 CE)

**Inka Empire (Tawantinsuyu):**
- Largest pre-Columbian state (Americas)
- **Extent:** Peru, Ecuador, Bolivia, Chile, Argentina (parts)
- 2 million+ km²
- 10 million+ people

**Capital: Cusco:**
- Sacred center
- Coricancha (Sun Temple, gold-covered)
- Massive stonework (no mortar)

**Machu Picchu:**
- Royal estate (Pachacuti?)
- Highlands (ridge)
- Terraces, temples, residences
- **Hiram Bingham** ("rediscovered" 1911, known locally)

**Administration:**
- **Decimal system** (hierarchy)
- Mit'a (labor tax)
- Quipu (census, tribute, records)
- **Quipucamayoc** (specialists)

**Roads:**
- 40,000 km (Qhapaq Ñan)
- Highlands, coast
- Suspension bridges
- Relay runners (chasqui)

**No Writing:**
- Quipu (not linguistic writing)
- Oral traditions

**No Wheel:**
- Llamas (pack animals, not draft)

**Religion:**
- Sun worship (Inti)
- Ancestor veneration (mummies)
- Capacocha (child sacrifice, mountains)

**Spanish Conquest:**
- Francisco Pizarro (1532)
- Civil war (Inka)
- Disease (smallpox, devastated)
- Capture of Atahualpa
- Fall of empire (1,572 CE, last stronghold)

## North America

### Eastern Woodlands

**Archaic Period (~8,000-1,000 BCE):**
- Foragers
- Domestication (local plants: sunflower, squash)

**Woodland Period (~1,000 BCE - 1,000 CE):**

**Adena (~800 BCE - 100 CE, Ohio Valley):**
- Burial mounds (earthworks)
- Grave goods (copper, mica)

**Hopewell (~200 BCE - 500 CE, Ohio, Illinois):**
- Elaborate earthworks (geometric, effigy mounds)
- Trade networks (wide: Gulf shells, Great Lakes copper, Rockies obsidian)
- Artistry (mica, copper)

**Mississippian Culture (~800-1,600 CE):**
- **Maize agriculture** (from Mesoamerica)
- **Cahokia** (~1,050-1,350 CE, Illinois)
  - Largest city (north of Mexico)
  - 10,000-20,000 people (peak)
  - **Monks Mound** (largest earthwork, North America)
    - 30 m high, 300 m × 200 m base
  - Plazas, palisade
  - Chiefdom (hierarchy)
  - Decline (~1,350 CE, reasons unclear)
- Other centers (Moundville, Etowah, Spiro)
- Ceremonial complexes

### Southwest

**Basketmaker (~200 BCE - 700 CE):**
- Ancestral Pueblo (Anasazi, outdated term)
- Pit houses
- Baskets (no pottery initially)

**Pueblo (~700-1,300 CE):**
- Pueblos (above-ground, stone/adobe)
- **Chaco Canyon (~850-1,150 CE, New Mexico):**
  - Great Houses (Pueblo Bonito)
  - Roads (straight, wide)
  - Astronomical alignments
  - Regional center (pilgrimage? Trade?)
  - Decline (~1,150 CE, drought)
- **Mesa Verde (~600-1,300 CE, Colorado):**
  - Cliff dwellings (Cliff Palace)
  - Abandoned ~1,300 CE (drought, "Great Drought")
- Kivas (ceremonial chambers, underground)

**Hohokam (~300-1,450 CE, Arizona):**
- **Irrigation canals** (extensive)
- Ball courts (Mesoamerican influence)
- Etched shells
- Declined (~1,450 CE)

**Descendants:**
- Modern Pueblo peoples (Hopi, Zuni, Acoma, Taos, etc.)
