## Overview: Multiple Routes Through Anthropology

The knowledge base is **non-linear**. There are many valid starting points depending on learner interests and goals.

**Core principle:** Meet learners where they are, guide them where they want to go.

## Learning Pathways

### Pathway 1: Traditional Academic (40-60 hours)
**Systematic coverage, foundations first**

1. Foundations (culture, relativism, holism, emic/etic, fieldwork, ethics)
2. Theoretical timeline (evolutionism ‚Üí functionalism ‚Üí interpretive ‚Üí contemporary)
3. Four fields overview
4. Cultural anthropology core (kinship, economics, politics, religion, gender, medical)
5. Biological anthropology (evolution, variation, primatology)
6. Archaeology (methods, prehistory, civilizations)
7. Linguistic anthropology (diversity, sociolinguistics, discourse)
8. Regional ethnographies
9. Topical specializations (urban, environment, digital)
10. Integration (cross-cutting themes, synthesis)

---

### Pathway 2: Contemporary Issues First (15-25 hours)
**Present backward to theory and history**

1. Pick contemporary issue (climate, migration, racial justice, pandemic, digital surveillance, urban inequality)
2. Relevant anthropological concepts
3. Theoretical frameworks (structural violence, political ecology, globalization, power/knowledge)
4. Historical context (colonialism, capitalism, state formation)
5. Comparative cases
6. Applied anthropology and activism

---

### Pathway 3: Ethnographic Stories (20-40 hours)
**Narratives first, concepts emerge**

1. Select vivid ethnographic examples
2. Extract concepts from stories
3. Build theoretical frameworks
4. Compare across cases
5. Contemporary applications
6. Expand to new ethnographies

---

### Pathway 4: Subfield Deep Dive (15-25 hours each)
**Focus on one area, connect to others**

**Medical:** Disease/illness, medical systems, structural violence, global health ‚Üí Connect to biology, politics, economics, religion

**Environmental:** Political ecology, TEK, climate change, Anthropocene ‚Üí Connect to politics, economics, indigenous knowledge, archaeology

**Digital:** Internet, algorithms, AI, platform capitalism ‚Üí Connect to economics, power, globalization, identity

**Urban:** Cities theory, inequality, gentrification, global cities ‚Üí Connect to migration, economics, environment, politics

---

### Pathway 5: Thematic Comparative (10-20 hours per theme)
**One theme across cultures**

**Marriage and Family:**
- Compare marriage rules worldwide
- Arranged vs. love marriages
- Polygyny, polyandry
- Same-sex marriage
- Contemporary transformations

**Ritual and Meaning:**
- Rites of passage across cultures
- Pilgrimage
- Festivals
- Ancestor worship
- Contemporary spirituality

**Exchange and Value:**
- Gift economies
- Market exchange
- Reciprocity
- Money systems
- Contemporary economies

---

### Pathway 6: Regional Focus (15-25 hours per region)
**Cultural diversity within one area**

**Options:**
- Africa (Nuer, Azande, Yoruba, Maasai, San)
- Asia (India caste, Japan work culture, China kinship, Southeast Asia)
- Indigenous Americas (Northwest Coast, Plains, Amazon, Mesoamerica, Inuit)
- Pacific (Melanesia exchange, Polynesia navigation, Australia Aboriginal)
- Middle East (Pastoralism, Islam, conflict)
- Europe (Post-socialist, migration, nationalism)

---

### Pathway 7: Theoretical Timeline (20-30 hours)
**Intellectual history of anthropology**

1. Origins: Evolutionism, diffusionism (1860-1920)
2. Classical: Functionalism, structuralism (1920-1960)
3. Interpretive/Critical: Symbolic, Marxist, feminist (1960-1990)
4. Contemporary: Practice theory, ontological turn, decolonial (1990-present)

---

### Pathway 8: Skills/Professional (10-20 hours)
**Practical applications**

1. Cultural competence (recognize assumptions, cross-cultural communication)
2. Research methods (participant observation, interviewing, ethics)
3. Regional preparation (specific cultural norms)
4. Applied contexts (business, development, healthcare, education, UX)

## Navigation Principles

**1. Follow Curiosity:** Interest = motivation

**2. Build Foundation:** Core concepts (culture, relativism, holism, emic/etic, fieldwork)

**3. Connect Domains:** Kinship + economics + politics + religion + gender all interrelate

**4. Use Examples:** Concepts + examples = understanding

**5. Adjust Depth:** Skim overview, deep dive where interested

**6. Spiral Back:** Revisit earlier topics with new knowledge

**7. Ask Questions:** Generate own, pursue answers

## Cross-Cutting Themes Connect Everything

**Power/Inequality:**
- Appears in kinship, economics, politics, gender, race, urban, environment, digital
- **Key concept:** Structural, relational, symbolic
- **Examples:** Patriarchy, class, racism, colonialism, gentrification, algorithmic bias

**Globalization/Transnationalism:**
- Appears in economics, politics, religion, urban, environment, digital, kinship
- **Key concept:** Flows across borders, uneven integration
- **Examples:** Migration, trade, climate change, internet, transnational families

**Agency/Structure:**
- Appears in ALL domains
- **Key concept:** Dialectical, mutually constitutive
- **Examples:** Practice theory, resistance, habitus, everyday politics

**Identity/Belonging:**
- Appears in ethnicity, nationalism, race, gender, religion, diaspora
- **Key concept:** Constructed, performed, relational
- **Examples:** Ethnic boundaries, imagined communities, racialization, gender performance

**Change/Continuity:**
- Appears in ALL domains
- **Key concept:** Always changing, patterns persist
- **Examples:** Globalization, technology, invention of tradition, strategic essentialism

## How Concepts Connect

**Culture** (central hub) ‚Üí
- Kinship (cultural organization of relatedness)
- Economics (cultural organization of production/exchange)
- Politics (cultural organization of power)
- Religion (cultural systems of meaning)
- Language (cultural communication)
- Gender (cultural construction of sex)
- Medical (cultural understanding of health)

**Four Fields** interconnect:
- Cultural ‚Üî Biological (bio-cultural, race, medical)
- Cultural ‚Üî Archaeological (material culture, ethnoarchaeology)
- Cultural ‚Üî Linguistic (language = culture, discourse)
- Biological ‚Üî Archaeological (bioarchaeology, evolution)

**Major domains** link:
- Kinship ‚Üí Economics (inheritance, exchange)
- Kinship ‚Üí Politics (lineages, succession)
- Economics ‚Üí Politics (class, taxation, redistribution)
- Politics ‚Üí Religion (legitimation, theocracy)
- Religion ‚Üí Healing (medical systems)
- Gender ‚Üí ALL (intersects everything)
