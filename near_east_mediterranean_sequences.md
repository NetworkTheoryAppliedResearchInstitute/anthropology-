# Near East and Mediterranean Archaeological Sequences

---

## Mesopotamia: Between the Rivers

### Geographic Context

**"Land Between Rivers":**
- Tigris and Euphrates
- Modern Iraq, parts of Syria, Turkey, Iran
- Fertile alluvium (flood deposits)
- No stone, timber, metal (imported)

**Regions:**
- **Sumer** (south)
- **Akkad** (north of Sumer)
- **Assyria** (far north, upper Tigris)
- **Babylonia** (central/south)

---

## Mesopotamian Chronology

### Ubaid Period (~6,500-4,000 BCE)

**Early Villages:**
- Tell al-'Ubaid (type site)
- Eridu (one of oldest cities)
- Irrigation begins
- Temples (small)
- Pottery (distinctive painted)

### Uruk Period (~4,000-3,100 BCE)

**Urbanization:**
- Uruk (largest city, 50,000+)
- Monumental architecture
- Temples (massive)
- **Writing emerges** (~3,300 BCE)
- Cylinder seals
- Long-distance trade
- Social stratification

### Early Dynastic Period (~2,900-2,334 BCE)

**City-States:**
- Ur, Lagash, Kish, Nippur, Uruk
- Competition, warfare
- Royal tombs (Ur, incredible wealth)
- **Leonard Woolley** (excavator, 1920s-30s)
  - Queen Puabi's tomb
  - Gold, lapis lazuli
  - Human sacrifice (retainers)

**Innovations:**
- Wheeled vehicles
- Potter's wheel
- Bronze metallurgy

### Akkadian Empire (~2,334-2,154 BCE)

**First Empire:**
- **Sargon of Akkad** (founder)
- Conquered Sumer
- Unified Mesopotamia
- Semitic language (Akkadian)
- Centralized administration

**Collapse:**
- ~2,154 BCE
- Gutian invasion
- Climate change? (drought)

### Ur III Dynasty (~2,112-2,004 BCE)

**Sumerian Revival:**
- Ur-Nammu (founder)
- Ziggurat of Ur (temple)
- Highly centralized
- Bureaucracy (thousands of tablets)
- Decline: Elamite invasion

### Old Babylonian Period (~2,000-1,600 BCE)

**Hammurabi (~1,792-1,750 BCE):**
- King of Babylon
- Empire builder
- **Code of Hammurabi**
  - Law code (282 laws)
  - Stele (stone pillar)
  - "Eye for an eye"
  - Class distinctions

### Kassite Period (~1,600-1,155 BCE)

- Foreign dynasty (Kassites)
- Long stability
- Decline obscure

### Assyrian Empire (~911-609 BCE)

**Neo-Assyrian:**
- Military power
- Conquered vast territories
- Nineveh (capital)
- Palace reliefs (vivid)
- Library of Ashurbanipal
  - Cuneiform tablets
  - *Epic of Gilgamesh* preserved

**Collapse:**
- 612 BCE (Nineveh sacked)
- Medes and Babylonians

### Neo-Babylonian Empire (~626-539 BCE)

**Nebuchadnezzar II (~605-562 BCE):**
- Babylon rebuilt (magnificent)
- Ishtar Gate (glazed bricks)
- Hanging Gardens? (legendary)
- Judean exile

**Conquest:**
- 539 BCE (Cyrus the Great, Persia)
- End of Mesopotamian independence

## Egypt: The Gift of the Nile

### Geographic Context

**The Nile:**
- Predictable floods (annual)
- Fertile silt
- Narrow valley (desert east/west)
- Circumscribed

**Regions:**
- **Upper Egypt** (south, Nile valley)
- **Lower Egypt** (north, Delta)

---

## Egyptian Chronology

### Predynastic Period (~5,000-3,100 BCE)

**Naqada Culture:**
- Villages → chiefdoms
- Social stratification
- Trade (Nubia, Levant)
- Painted pottery
- Ceremonial mace heads

### Early Dynastic Period (~3,100-2,686 BCE)

**Dynasties 1-2:**
- **Unification** (~3,100 BCE)
  - Narmer (Menes?)
  - Upper + Lower Egypt
  - Narmer Palette (iconography)
- Memphis (capital)
- Mastaba tombs (elite)
- Writing (hieroglyphics developed)

### Old Kingdom (~2,686-2,181 BCE)

**Dynasties 3-6:**
- **"Pyramid Age"**
- Centralized state
- Divine kingship (pharaoh = god)

**Pyramids:**
- Step Pyramid (Djoser, Saqqara, ~2,650 BCE)
  - Imhotep (architect)
  - First stone monument (massive)
- **Great Pyramids (Giza, ~2,550-2,490 BCE):**
  - Khufu (Cheops), largest
  - Khafre, Menkaure
  - Sphinx
  - Engineering marvel
  - Labor organization (not slaves—farmers, off-season)

**Collapse:**
- ~2,181 BCE
- First Intermediate Period
- Decentralization

### Middle Kingdom (~2,055-1,650 BCE)

**Dynasties 11-12:**
- Reunification
- Classical period (literature)
- Expansion (Nubia)
- Trade networks

**Decline:**
- Second Intermediate Period (~1,650-1,550 BCE)
- Hyksos (foreign rulers, Delta)
- Introduced horse, chariot

### New Kingdom (~1,550-1,077 BCE)

**Dynasties 18-20:**
- **Imperial Egypt**
- Expansion (Levant, Nubia)
- Wealth, power peak

**Famous Pharaohs:**

**Hatshepsut (~1,479-1,458 BCE):**
- Female pharaoh
- Peaceful reign
- Mortuary temple (Deir el-Bahari)
- Trade expedition (Punt)

**Akhenaten (~1,353-1,336 BCE):**
- Religious revolution
- Aten (sun disk) monotheism?
- Amarna (new capital)
- Art style radical
- Restoration after death

**Tutankhamun (~1,332-1,323 BCE):**
- Boy king
- Died young
- Tomb intact (Howard Carter, 1922)
- Incredible treasure

**Ramesses II (~1,279-1,213 BCE):**
- Long reign
- Builder (Abu Simbel, Ramesseum)
- Battle of Kadesh (Hittites)
- Exodus pharaoh? (debated)

**Decline:**
- Sea Peoples (~1,200 BCE)
- Economic troubles
- Third Intermediate Period

### Late Period (~664-332 BCE)

**Dynasties 26-31:**
- Foreign domination (Assyrians, Persians)
- Saite Renaissance (briefly)

### Ptolemaic Period (~332-30 BCE)

**After Alexander:**
- Greek dynasty (Ptolemies)
- Alexandria (capital)
- Lighthouse, Library
- **Cleopatra VII** (last pharaoh)
- Roman conquest (30 BCE)

## The Levant: Crossroads

### Geographic Context

**Modern:**
- Israel, Palestine, Lebanon, Jordan, western Syria

**Strategic:**
- Land bridge (Africa ↔ Asia)
- Trade routes
- Cultural crossroads

---

## Levantine Sequences

### Neolithic (~10,000-4,500 BCE)

**Natufian (~12,500-9,500 BCE):**
- Hunter-gatherers
- Sedentary (early)
- Circular houses
- Gazelle hunting

**Pre-Pottery Neolithic (~9,500-6,000 BCE):**
- **Jericho** (one of oldest towns)
  - Stone tower (~8,000 BCE)
  - Plastered skulls (ancestor veneration?)
  - Kathleen Kenyon (excavator)
- Ain Ghazal (Jordan)
  - Plaster statues (anthropomorphic)

**Pottery Neolithic (~6,000-4,500 BCE):**
- Agriculture established

### Bronze Age (~3,600-1,200 BCE)

**Early Bronze (~3,600-2,000 BCE):**
- Urban centers
- Fortifications
- Egypt/Mesopotamia contact

**Middle Bronze (~2,000-1,550 BCE):**
- Canaanite city-states
- Trade networks

**Late Bronze (~1,550-1,200 BCE):**
- Egyptian control
- Amarna Letters (diplomacy)
- **Ugarit** (Syria)
  - Palace archives
  - Alphabetic cuneiform

**Collapse:**
- ~1,200 BCE
- Sea Peoples
- Cities destroyed

### Iron Age (~1,200-586 BCE)

**Philistines:**
- Coastal cities
- Sea Peoples origin?
- Pottery (Aegean connections)

**Israelites/Judahites:**
- Highland villages
- United Monarchy? (Saul, David, Solomon)
  - Biblical narrative
  - Archaeological evidence limited
- Divided kingdoms (Israel, Judah)

**Conquest:**
- 722 BCE (Israel: Assyrians)
- 586 BCE (Judah: Babylonians, Jerusalem destroyed)

## Anatolia: Hittites and Beyond

### Geographic Context

**Modern Turkey:**
- Plateau (high, arid center)
- Coasts (Mediterranean, Aegean, Black Sea)

---

## Anatolian Sequences

### Neolithic (~7,000-5,500 BCE)

**Çatalhöyük:**
- Central Anatolia
- Large settlement (3,500-8,000 people)
- Dense, no streets
- Enter through roofs
- Murals, plastered skulls
- James Mellaart (1960s), Ian Hodder (1990s+)

### Chalcolithic (~5,500-3,000 BCE)

- Copper use begins

### Early Bronze Age (~3,000-2,000 BCE)

**Troy I-V:**
- Northwest Anatolia
- Heinrich Schliemann (1870s, controversial)
- Fortified citadel
- Trade

### Hittite Empire (~1,600-1,200 BCE)

**Capital: Hattusa:**
- Central Anatolia
- Massive walls
- Temples, palaces
- Cuneiform archives

**Language:**
- Hittite (Indo-European)
- Deciphered (Bedřich Hrozný, 1915)

**Power:**
- Rival to Egypt
- Battle of Kadesh (Ramesses II)
- Ironworking (early adopters)

**Collapse:**
- ~1,200 BCE
- Sea Peoples
- Fragmentation

---

## The Aegean: Minoans and Mycenaeans

### Minoan Civilization (~3,000-1,100 BCE)

**Crete:**

**Palaces (~2,000-1,450 BCE):**
- Knossos (largest)
  - Arthur Evans (excavator, 1900+)
  - Frescoes (bull-leaping, dolphins)
  - "Labyrinth" (complex layout)
- Phaistos, Malia, Zakros

**Economy:**
- Trade (wide network)
- Olive oil, wine, pottery

**Religion:**
- Bull symbolism
- Goddess figures
- Peak sanctuaries

**Writing:**
- Linear A (~1,800-1,450 BCE)
  - **Undeciphered** (language unknown)

**Thera Eruption (~1,600 BCE):**
- Volcanic explosion (Santorini)
- Massive destruction
- Ash layers
- Akrotiri (buried city, "Pompeii of Aegean")

**Decline:**
- ~1,450 BCE
- Mycenaean takeover

### Mycenaean Civilization (~1,600-1,100 BCE)

**Mainland Greece:**

**Palaces:**
- Mycenae (most famous)
  - Heinrich Schliemann (excavator)
  - "Mask of Agamemnon" (actually earlier)
  - Cyclopean walls (massive stones)
- Pylos, Tiryns, Thebes

**Tholos Tombs:**
- Beehive-shaped
- Elite burials
- Treasury of Atreus (Mycenae)

**Linear B (~1,450-1,200 BCE):**
- Syllabic script
- **Deciphered** (Michael Ventris, 1952)
- **Archaic Greek!**
- Administrative (inventories, rations)
- No literature

**Warrior Society:**
- Fortifications
- Weapons (bronze swords, armor)
- Chariots

**Collapse (~1,200 BCE):**
- Palaces destroyed
- Writing lost
- **Dark Age** (~1,200-800 BCE)
  - Population decline
  - Simpler society
  - Oral tradition (*Iliad*, *Odyssey* roots?)
